Metric¶

Kerr metric.

Returns:

Notes

From the Kerr lagrangian:

\[\begin{split}\begin{eqnarray} \mathscr{L}(x^\mu,\dot{x}^\mu)&=&\frac{1}{2}g_{\mu\nu}\dot{x}^\mu\dot{x}^\nu\\ &=&\frac{1}{2}\left[-\left(1-\frac{2r}{\Sigma}\right)\dot{t}^2-\frac{4ar\sin^2\theta}{\Sigma}\dot{t}\dot{\phi}\right.\\ & &\left.+\frac{\Sigma}{\Delta}\dot{r}^2+\frac{\Sigma}{1}\dot{\theta}^2+\left(r^2+a^2+\frac{2a^2r\sin^2\theta}{\Sigma}\right)\sin^2\theta\,\dot{\phi}^2\right]\\ \end{eqnarray}\end{split}\]
\[\Sigma\equiv r^2+a^2\cos^2\theta\]
\[\Delta\equiv r^2-2r+a^2\]

initial conditions are:

\[\begin{split}\left\{ \begin{eqnarray} p_t\equiv\frac{\partial\mathscr{L}}{\partial\dot{t}}&=&-E\\ p_r\equiv\frac{\partial\mathscr{L}}{\partial\dot{r}}&=&\frac{\Sigma}{\Delta}\dot{r}\\ p_\theta\equiv\frac{\partial\mathscr{L}}{\partial\dot{\theta}}&=&\frac{\Sigma}{1}\dot{\theta}\\ p_\phi\equiv\frac{\partial\mathscr{L}}{\partial\dot{\phi}}&=&+L_z\\ \end{eqnarray} \right.\end{split}\]

where (see [1]):

\[E=\sqrt{\frac{\Sigma -2r}{\Sigma\Delta}\left(\Sigma\dot{r}^2+\Sigma\Delta\dot{\theta}^2+\Delta\mu\right)+\Delta\sin^2\theta\,\dot{\phi}^2}\]
\[L_z=\left[\frac{\Sigma\Delta\dot{\phi}-2arE}{\Sigma-2r}\right]\sin^2\theta\]
\[Q=p_\theta^2+\left[\frac{L_z^2}{\sin^2\theta}+a^2(\mu-E^2)\right]\cos^2\theta\]
\[\kappa=Q+L_z^2-a^2(\mu-E^2)\]

\(a\equiv\frac{L}{M}\) is the Kerr parameter (conventionally, \(M=1\)), \(L_z\) is the projection of the particle angular momentum along the black hole spin axis and \(Q\) the Carter constant and \(\kappa\) another constant that is always non-negative.

In the black hole system coordinate, initial cartesian velocities \((\dot{x},\dot{y},\dot{z})\) are determined by differentiating kerr.coord.obs_to_bh() along the photon arrival direction (= z direction):

\[\begin{split}\left\{ \begin{eqnarray} \dot{x}&=&-\sin\theta_\text{obs}\cos\phi_\text{obs}\\ \dot{y}&=&-\sin\theta_\text{obs}\sin\phi_\text{obs}\\ \dot{z}&=&-\cos\theta_\text{obs}\\ \end{eqnarray} \right.\end{split}\]

Then, initial spherical velocities \((\dot{r},\dot{\theta},\dot{\phi})\) are determined by differentiating \((r,\theta,\phi)\) = kerr.coord.cartesian_to_boyer_lindquist() and substituting \((x,y,z)\to\) kerr.coord.boyer_lindquist_to_cartesian() and \((\dot{x},\dot{y},\dot{z})\):

\[\begin{split}\left\{ \begin{matrix} \dot{r}=\frac{\partial r}{\partial x}\dot{x}+\frac{\partial r}{\partial y}\dot{y}+\frac{\partial r}{\partial z}\dot{z}\\ \dot{\theta}=\frac{\partial\theta}{\partial x}\dot{x}+\frac{\partial\theta}{\partial y}\dot{y}+\frac{\partial\theta}{\partial z}\dot{z}\\ \dot{\phi}=\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial x}\dot{x}+\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial y}\dot{y}+\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial z}\dot{z}\\ \end{matrix} \right|_{\text{subs. }(x,y,z)\text{ and }(\dot{x},\dot{y},\dot{z})}=\left\{ \begin{matrix} -\frac{r\mathcal{R}\sin\theta\sin\theta_\text{obs}\cos\Phi+\mathcal{R}^2\cos\theta\cos\theta_\text{obs}}{\Sigma}\\ +\frac{r\sin\theta\cos\theta_\text{obs}-\mathcal{R}\cos\theta\sin\theta_\text{obs}\cos\Phi}{\Sigma}\\ \frac{\sin\theta_\text{obs}\sin\Phi}{\mathcal{R}\sin\theta}\\ \end{matrix} \right.\end{split}\]

where \(\mathcal{R}\equiv\sqrt{r^2+a^2}\) and \(\Phi\equiv\phi-\phi_\text{obs}\).

Equations are:

\[\dot{r}=p_r\times\frac{\Delta}{\Sigma}\]
\[\dot{\theta}=p_\theta\times\frac{1}{\Sigma}\]
\[\dot{\phi}=\frac{2arE+(\Sigma-2r)\frac{L_z}{\sin^2\theta}}{\Sigma\Delta}\]

\[\dot{p_\theta}=\frac{\sin\theta\cos\theta}{\Sigma}\left[\frac{L_z^2}{\sin^4\theta}+a^2(\mu-E^2)\right]\]
